About Composting

Did you know?

  • Composting is a way of utilizing microorganisms to break down and decompose organic matter.
  • 70% of your waste stream can be composted or recycled.
  • An average household produces 600 pounds of compostable yard waste yearly.
  • Compostable material makes up 20 percent of a community's residential waste.
  • Compost needs between 60 to 90 days minimum to complete the process, although optimal ranges are between 90 to 120 days.

Benefits of composting

  • Reduction of waste needlessly going into landfills.
  • Production of an organic soil amendment rich in nutrients that will help produce healthier plants.
  • Compost:
    • Enhances germination, fuels plant growth and improves yields of crops
    • Balances the pH and moisture of the soil so that plants grow better and lawns stay greener
    • Helps to diminish erosion, which is often attributed to low fertility
    • Increases soil permeability and decreases the need for irrigation and fertilization
  • The process of composting kills pathogens. This takes away the need for pesticides thus protecting the ecosystem.
  • By reducing the amount of organic matter in the landfills, we can bring the level of greenhouse gases down.

How does the program work?
The main objective is to connect:

  • People who would like to compost (e.g. apartment dwellers) but don't have space to hold it.
  • People who would like compost to fertilize their gardens or crops.

How can I participate?

  • Go to grasshopper.ca/composte
  • Create a profile and update your compost needs
  • Review your match (the website will provide you with options around your neighborhood so that you can connect with the match most suitable for you)
